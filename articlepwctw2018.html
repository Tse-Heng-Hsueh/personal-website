2018 PwC Taiwan Summer Internship Application
Updated Sep 17, 2018
The application period for summer internships/part-time positions at the four major accounting firms in Taiwan usually starts in early April and ends at the end of April. The exact timing may vary each year, but it generally falls within this timeframe. If you are an accounting major and interested in applying for an internship at one of these firms, the accounting department of your university will usually share relevant information, so make sure to check your emails frequently. If you are not an accounting major but still want to apply, pay attention to the firms' websites during April, or you can ask your accounting major friends for assistance.

This year (2018), except for Ernst & Young (EY), the other three major firms only opened audit internship positions to external applicants. Why do we say "external"? That's because there are different ways to obtain an internship at an accounting firm. For example, if you win competitions organized by these firms (e.g., EY International Tax Elite Competition, PwC International Tax Cup Case Analysis Competition), you may have a chance to secure an internship, although such opportunities usually focus on tax-related positions. Alternatively, if you have attended seminars and established friendly relationships with people from the firms, not necessarily high-ranking individuals, they might be willing to recommend you. There are actually many approaches, but the key is to network and inquire extensively. However, for regular individuals like myself, it is still possible to obtain an internship through open recruitment processes.

Now, let's talk about Ernst & Young (EY). EY is one of the few firms that conduct open recruitment for interns during winter break, and they offer not just audit internships but also tax, transaction advisory, and enterprise risk management internships, both during winter and summer breaks. If you're interested, you can visit the 2018 EY Summer Internship Program website at https://www.ey.com/tw/zh_tw/careers/students/ey-summer-internship-program-2018. If the link is still active, you can find detailed qualifications for each department's recruitment.

In summary, the qualification requirements for EY's audit, tax, transaction advisory, and enterprise risk management positions are not significantly different from those of the other three major firms. The requirements are as follows: you should be a third-year student who has completed intermediate accounting and auditing courses. However, these two criteria are not absolute. If you are a second-year or even a first-year student, I strongly recommend giving it a try. As long as you can prove your capabilities or demonstrate your dedication, it's worth a shot. Opportunities are never given if you don't try. Apart from the aforementioned department-specific positions, EY also offers a "Performance Improvement" internship that has higher requirements, including SQL programming skills, experience in macro programming development, and proficiency in English. If you're interested, you may consider taking relevant courses in the upcoming academic year.

Finally, let's talk about the application process. You need to fill out the application forms provided by each of the four major firms, attach your academic transcripts from previous years, and send them to the designated addresses or submit them to your university's accounting department, depending on whether the firms have collaborations with accounting departments. If not, you'll need to send the application by yourself. Additionally, in most cases, interviews are not required for internships at accounting firms.

As for why the title mentions PwC, it's because out of the four major firms, only PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) offered me an internship. So, I will be spending my entire summer at PwC. I used to wonder how I would choose if more than one firm accepted me, but as it turned out, I was overthinking it.

Thank you!!
