2018 PwC China Winter Internship Application
Updated Sep 01, 2018
As a student majoring in accounting at a national university, the information I come across is often related to accounting, which naturally gives me a better understanding of the accounting industry.

To give a brief introduction, there are four major accounting firm alliances globally: PwC, Deloitte, KPMG, and E&Y. Currently, the largest accounting firm in Taiwan is Ernst & Young (EY), which is part of the Deloitte alliance and operates as a member firm within its global network. Therefore, we often refer to Ernst & Young as Deloitte. Other examples include PwC Taiwan, which is affiliated with PricewaterhouseCoopers, KPMG Taiwan, and EY Taiwan.

Internationally, it is common to use the format of "Alliance + Region" to refer to these firms, such as Deloitte US or Deloitte Taiwan. It is important to understand that communication and understanding are the key, and there is no right or wrong way to refer to them, just different conventions.

As I am about to go on an exchange program at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, I applied for the 2018 PwC China Winter Internship Program. When applying for the exchange, I chose Mainland China with the intention of experiencing life in Shanghai and exploring the job market there. After confirming my acceptance to Shanghai Jiao Tong University, I started applying for internships.

The application process for PwC was similar to that of other large companies, consisting of two main stages: an online test and an interview. What was unique is that I did not obtain internship information through the official PwC China channel. I saw someone reposting the information in a WeChat internship exchange group. Therefore, before applying, I decided to chat with that student to confirm the company culture and job responsibilities. He also helped me with a recommendation when I made the final decision to apply (which is somewhat similar to what is known as "referral"). However, whether there was a referral or not did not affect the subsequent two stages, as I still had to go through the online test and interview.

The online test consisted of five sections: logic, math, Chinese reading comprehension, English, and personal traits. After passing the online assessment, the interview was scheduled through phone or email.

The online test was not too difficult, somewhat similar to personality tests we did in high school and English reading comprehension tests like the ones in college entrance exams. It took about 2.5 hours to complete. I remember needing to have a stable internet connection, as my internet disconnected twice during the English section, causing delays...

The interview was not as challenging as I had imagined. It lasted approximately 20 minutes, and they asked questions based on my resume to understand my background. I believe the main focus was on assessing how well I would fit in with their team. I would like to mention that I applied to the newly established Innovation Services Department at PwC China Shanghai. As for interviews in other departments, such as auditing or tax, I am not quite familiar with their procedures.

I hope to have the opportunity to share more about my life as an exchange student and intern in the future. Thank you. Feel free to leave any questions in the comments section below!
